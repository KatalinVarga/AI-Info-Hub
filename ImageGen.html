Algorithmic Visual Design

AI image generation has evolved significantly in recent years, allowing users to create realistic or artistic visuals simply by providing text descriptions. (Some AI models are multimodal, accepting images as prompts or a combination of text and images simultaneously.)

Limitations of AI Visuals

Common Errors

Blurry images

Lack of detail, (e.g. lack of facial features), low resolution. Resolution is usually better on paid subscription and most models allow enhancing pictures for extra credits.

Anatomical Distortions

Extra teeth, fingers, nostrils, limbs, or misshapen limbs, missing limbs, missing iris, etc.; incorrect placement of body parts.

Blending and Merging

Blending artifacts at object boundaries, color bleeding between objects, pattern bleeding between objects.

Broken Physics

Perspective and spatial inconsistencies, wrong proportions for objects. Illogical object interactions and physics (e.g. gravitiy does not apply to an object and it hangs in the air). Background/foreground misalignment.

Light and Shadow Distortions

Unrealistic lighting and shadows.Poor handling of reflections and transparency.

Symmetry and Pattern Imbalance

AI images struggle with symmetry issues, creating either unnaturally perfect mirroring or chaotic element distribution. This causes overcrowded compositions and artificial balance lacking natural variation. Most models, however, cannot produce intentional seamless patterns when requested.
(Exception: Flux Pro.)

Hallucinations

Phantom elements like duplicate heads, unexpected additional characters, miniature versions of subjects, or entirely unrequested objects appearing in the composition. Also: watermark and signature hallucinations.

Text rendering issues

AI image generators often fail at text rendering, producing misspelled words, random characters, or illegible gibberish instead of coherent writing.
( Ideogram is good at rendering text on generated images.)

Counting Errors

Some models struggle with object counts. For example, requests for a bear and a rabbit may result in extra animals or merged figures. Specifying a number of objects seldom results in the exact amount. (Also, some parts of your prompt may be ignored anyway.)

Prompting 101

Text-to-Image

  • Use detailed, descriptive language, mention the background elements, too (e.g., "A serene mountain lake at sunset with pink and orange clouds reflected in crystal clear water")
  • Include multiple adjectives to convey mood and style preferences
  • Specify artistic styles (like watercolor, pixel art) or artists (e.g., "in the style of Van Gogh")
  • Include technical parameters (e.g., "8K resolution, photorealistic lighting")
  • Use qualitative modifiers (e.g., "cinematic, dramatic, hyperdetailed")

Image (with text prompt)-to-image

  • Start with a reference image and describe desired modifications
  • Specify elements to preserve and elements to change
  • Avoid words/objects that the model struggles with (e.g. teeth)
  • Use masking to isolate areas for targeted changes
  • Use a conversational AI to help refine prompts for better results
  • Test different prompts and refine based on the outcomes

Additional Prompting Tips

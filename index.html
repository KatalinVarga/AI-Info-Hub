Understanding Artificial Intelligence

Explore the world of AI, from its history and concepts to applications, risks, and useful tools

What is Artificial Intelligence?

According to Google Cloud:

Artificial intelligence is a field of science concerned with building computers and machines that can reason, learn, and act in such a way that would normally require human intelligence or that involves data whose scale exceeds what humans can analyse.

AI is a broad field that encompasses many different disciplines, including:

  • Computer science
  • Data analytics and statistics
  • Hardware and software engineering
  • Linguistics
  • Neuroscience
  • Philosophy and psychology
  • Business use: data analytics, predictions and forecasting, object categorisation, natural language processing, recommendations, intelligent data retrieval, and more

Not included in Google's definition, but:

  • AI is also used for generative ART – literature, images, videos, design, music, etc.

History of AI

AI used to be a conceptual idea in academic papers. Today it's a transformative force shaping industries, society, and individual lives.

Ancient Philosophy

Even some ancient philosophers developed somewhat mechanistic worldviews (e.g. Empedocles, c. 494-434 BCE)

Descartes (1637)

René Descartes proposed a test for machine intelligence based on language use

Ada Lovelace (1842-1843)

Ada Lovelace stated that machines couldn't "originate anything"

Turing Test (1950)

Alan Turing proposed the famous Turing Test and challenged Lovelace's assertion, suggesting that machines could potentially think

Term "Artificial Intelligence" (1955)

John McCarthy coined the term "Artificial Intelligence"

Rule-Based Systems (1960s-70s)

Early AI systems relied on symbolic reasoning (e.g. ELIZA, or Optical Character Recognition – OCR- software for scanning and reading documents for data entry)

Expert Systems (1970s-80s)

Programs working with knowledge-bases and relying on handcrafted rules (e.g. MYCIN)

Machine Learning

Machine Learning is a form of algorithmic learning. There are many categorisations of ML.

Based on Learning Methods:

Supervised Learning

Learning from labeled data where the model is trained on input-output pairs.

Unsupervised Learning

Learning from unlabeled data to find patterns or structure.

Semi-supervised Learning

Combines a small amount of labeled data with a large amount of unlabeled data.

Self-supervised Learning

Models learn from the data itself by predicting parts of the input.

Reinforcement Learning

Learning through interaction with an environment to maximise rewards.

Deep Learning

Using neural networks with multiple layers to learn from data.

Probabilistic Models

Probabilistic models in AI represent uncertainty in data and predict outcomes using probability distributions.

AI Learning from AI

AI learning from AI is an emerging field:

  • Transfer Learning
  • Model Distillation (e.g. OpenAI claims that DeepSeek "distilled" knowledge from OpenAI's models)
  • Collaborative Learning
  • Meta-Learning

Deep Learning in Detail

  • Deep learning models use multiple layers of interconnected nodes (like our brains)
  • Learning process is automatic, minimal human intervention
  • Large data requirements (for training)
  • Identify patterns in data
  • Wide range of applications (e.g. image recognition, natural language processing, speech recognition, and autonomous vehicles, generative art)

Deep Learning Models

  • Multilayer Perceptrons, e.g. stock market prediction
  • Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), used in Google Lens, self-driving cars, security cameras, Face ID on Apple devices
  • Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), e.g. Apple's Siri, Google Assistant
  • Transformer models, like:
  • LLMs used in NLP (Open OpenAI's GPT, Google Translate, Grammarly, Gemini, Copilot, Google Search)
  • Image and video generation, e.g. DALL-E, Parti, Muse, and Phenaki
  • Speech recognition, like Conformer and Whisper
  • Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), e.g.: Google Maps, Social network analysis, recommendation systems, drug discovery, Pinterest
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP): Tools like OpenAI's GPT, Google Translate, Grammarly, Jasper, Otter.ai, Replika, Gemini, Copilot
  • Generative Models:
  • Variational Autoencoders (VAEs)
  • Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) - DALL-E, Midjourney, Runway ML
  • Diffusion Models (Stable Diffusion)
  • Variational Autoencoders (VAEs), such as Artbreeder
  • Capsule Networks, e.g. specific medical imaging tasks

Applications of AI

"There's an App AI for that"

Business and Commerce

  • Recommendation Systems: AI algorithms analyse consumer behavior to provide personalised product recommendations on platforms like Netflix, LinkedIn, and Spotify
  • Customer Service: AI-powered chatbots and virtual assistants handle customer inquiries
  • Fraud Detection: AI systems help identify and prevent fraudulent activities in financial transactions

Personal Assistance and Smart Devices

  • Virtual Assistants: Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant, and Microsoft Copilot use natural language processing (NLP) to understand and respond to user queries
  • Smartphone Features: AI powers facial recognition, voice assistants, and camera enhancements in smartphones

Transportation and Navigation

  • Autonomous Vehicles: AI systems process data from sensors and cameras to navigate roads
  • Navigation Apps: real-time traffic updates and optimised routes

Healthcare

  • Diagnostic imaging analysis, radiography
  • AI-based symptom checkers
  • Infectious disease tracking and analysis
  • Disease detection (e.g. some forms of cancer)
  • Treatment planning, health monitoring
  • Patient risk prediction
  • Remote healthcare (e.g. wearables)
  • Drug discovery
  • AI used in surgery (e.g. helping robotic arms or providing haptic feedback to surgeon)

Manufacturing and Industry

  • Quality Control: AI systems inspect products for defects
  • Predictive Maintenance: AI predicts when equipment needs maintenance, reducing downtime
  • Supply Chain Optimisation: AI improves logistics and inventory management

Content Creation and Media

  • Text Generation: Human-like text for various purposes
  • Image and Video Manipulation: AI tools can edit, enhance, and even create synthetic media
  • Generative audio (sound/voice/music)
  • Generative Art

Risks with AI

Data-related Risks

  • Bias and discrimination: AI models can perpetuate or amplify existing biases in training data
  • Data poisoning: malicious actors can corrupt training data
  • Privacy violations: AI systems may memorise and leak sensitive information from training datasets

AI System Related Risks

  • Lack of transparency: the decision-making processes of AI systems can be opaque
  • Overreliance on AI: excessive dependence on AI systems can lead to errors and reputational damage/ diminished human skills and decision-making abilities
  • Uncontrolled AI development: losing human control over AI systems

Cybersecurity and Misuse

  • Cybersecurity threats: sophisticated cyberattacks, voice cloning, identity theft
  • Deepfakes / fake news, misinformation overload
  • Use for criminal activity
  • Biological weapon development

Economic and Societal Impacts

  • Job displacement
  • Financial instability: AI-driven algorithmic trading could potentially trigger market volatility and financial crises
  • Copyright and intellectual property issues

Sector-specific Risks

  • Healthcare: misdiagnosis
  • Self-driving vehicles: mistakes of AI may cost lives/ ethical dilemmas (whose lives to protect?)
  • Media: overload of inferior quality generated articles
  • Death of human art?

Environmental Impact

  • Energy consumption:
    • AI is energy hungry (training and running models) – Google ordered six or seven small nuclear reactors for its datacentres
    • Some estimates suggest that a single ChatGPT query consumes between 10 and 30 times the energy of a traditional internet search
  • However:
    • Some researchers are working on a project to give Energy Star ratings to different AI models
    • There's a lot of effort put into developing low-carbon AI models
    • AI can help mitigate climate change

AGI Risk

Emergence of AGI (Artificial General Intelligence) – some say this is the most serious risk. The AI robots are here (Ameca from Cornwall, Tesla's Optimus, Boston Dynamics’ Atlas, Figure 2, Unitree Robotics' robot).

  • It helps us stay relevant
  • Automates repetitive tasks
  • Optimises processes
  • It can be FUN:

How to use AI tools?

  • Beware hallucinations – fact-check!
  • Be careful with copyright:
  • The AI tool might not give you copyright at all for the generated content (e.g. Bing Image Creator)
  • Free tools might give you limited copyright, e.g. for personal use only
  • For commercial use, most tools require a paid subscription
  • T & C's change over time
DeepDream generated image
  • Even if you have all the necessary copyright for the tool, you could still breach copyright laws, if:
  • The training data was not solely on licensed content
  • The prompt you are using mentions artists names and style that might result in output too closely resembling their work (some models, like Bing Image Creator won't allow this at all)
  • The legal framework around this area is continuously evolving and what is allowed today, may not be tomorrow
